Mentoring Groups

We are excited to be starting a brand new group mentoring initiative and we're looking for candidates to join our spring session. At Girl Dev, we greatly value the importance of mentoring, continuous learning and building upon one's skill set. We also recognize the power of learning in small groups. Small groups enable its members to better understand the work through peer-assisted learning. We all know when you help someone else with a problem you often discover something new yourself, and that's the whole idea! The group mentor-ship model enhances your learning experience and teaches you to learn collaboratively, explain your findings and motivate one another to go further.

We are seven mentors who are excited to share our knowledge with those who are willing to learn! The groups will have a maximum size of four and the mentor-ship will span over three months. There will be a final deliverable which will be the result of the collaboration between all the different groups.

The topics that we will be mentoring are the following:

  • HTML and CSS > mentored by Steve Mynett
  • JavaScript and JQuery > mentored by Gabriel Gosselin
  • Databases and Big Data > mentored by Saba El-Hilo
  • Java > mentored by Cheryl Li and Jessica Johnson
  • Ruby > mentored by Francois Deschenes
  • The non technical side of software development > mentored by Noel Pullen

To join us you must be self motivated, driven to learn, committed, excited about working with a small group and learning from your peers and mentor. We are also focusing on female developers who are at an intermediate or senior level. Check out Ladies Learning Code for introductory level courses, and hopefully we'll see you here afterwards.

So if this sounds like you please complete this assessment so we can learn more about you and your skill level.

If you have any questions or you are interested in mentoring you can contact me Saba El-Hilo . You can also visit our meetup page for more information about the group.
